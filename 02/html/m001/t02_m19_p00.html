Identification

The engine drains

  • Consist of a system of pipe lines that carry leaking or overflowing fuel and oil from various engine assemblies and components during normal and abnormal running of the engine.
  • The drain system lines originate from different locations on the engine and terminate at 3 principal drain positions outside the engine.
  • On an installed engine, the drains terminate from the engine to the atmosphere through the cowling.
  • The 3 principal engine drains are:
    • Gang drain
    • Combustor drain
    • C-sump drain
  • The gang drain:
    • Located at the 6 o’clock position on the engine, aft of the fan module.
    • Terminates to the atmosphere at the drain mast through the lower door of the core cowl.
  • The combustor drain
    • Located at 6 o’clock position on the engine, just in front of the mount ring.
    • Terminates to the atmosphere through an opening in the exhaust nozzle fairing.
  • The C-sump drain
    • Located at the 6 o’clock exhaust frame strut, aft of the mount ring.
    • Terminates to the atmosphere through an opening in the exhaust nozzle fairing.

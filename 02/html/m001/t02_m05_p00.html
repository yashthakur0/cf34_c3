Identification

  • The engine safety hazard areas refer to the regions around a running engine that should be avoided to prevent injury to maintenance personnel and damage to the engine.
  • Injury and damage can occur due to the effects of suction and exhaust of a running engine.
  • A running engine has enough suction power to pull personnel, tools and equipment into it.
  • The engine exhaust includes:
    • discharge air that flows out from the fan discharge
    • exhaust gases that flow out of the exhaust nozzle
    • exhaust gases that escape during the operation of the APU.

  • The dimensions of the engine safety hazard areas depend on the following conditions:
    • Engine operation at maximum thrust
    • Engine operation at Idle thrust
    • Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) operation

  • With the engine operating at maximum thrust
    • Suction area of an engine is shaped like an arc of approximately 25 ft radius extending from the fan intake area.
    • Exhaust area extends rearwards from the exhaust nozzle up to a distance of approx 170 ft. The width of the exhaust area increases from the exhaust nozzle and reaches a maximum value of approx 18 ft.
    • Temperature and velocity of exhaust gases is maximum (>990 F, >544 mph) at the exhaust nozzle and reduces as the distance increases.

  • At idle thrust:
    • Suction area arc has a radius of approx 12 ft.
    • Exhaust area extends rearwards from the exhaust nozzle up to a distance of approx 100 ft. The maximum width of the idle exhaust area is approximately 18 ft.
    • Temperature and velocity of the exhaust gases is maximum (>740 F, >136 mph) at the exhaust nozzle and reduces as the distance increases.

  • With the APU in operation, the exhaust area from the APU extends rearwards in an angular arc of approx 15 ft radius.

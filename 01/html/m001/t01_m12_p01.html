Purpose

The compressor front frame is the first major structural support and has the engine waterwash capability built in by providing a system of spray nozzles and manifold hoses attached to the frame. They are connected together with a flexible hose leading to a single point connector located at the lower right hand side of the AGB. This system is used to clean the compressor for performance recovery. The 14 stages of blades on the rotor assembly compress the air before entering the combustion chamber. The compressor stator provides a casing for the rotor assembly and contains the IGV and stages 1-5 variable vanes and the stage 6 to 14 fixed vanes. The power takeoff assembly is splined to the compressor rotor and drives the AGB through a radial drive shaft to the AGB.

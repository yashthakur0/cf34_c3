Purpose

The compressor stator assembly provides a casing for the compressor rotor and directs air through each stage by the use of both variable and fixed guide vanes. The case is split on a horizontal line to allow for top case removal for repairs to the compressor rotor. Variable stator vanes are installed in the inlet guide vane and stages 1-5 through the compressor stator case. The fuel control moves all stages simultaneously through two actuators attached to two actuating shafts installed on the case halves. Stages 6-13 are fixed within the case halves. The compressor case has two bleed manifolds that draw seventh and tenth stage bleed air from the engine for carbon seal pressurization and aircraft use.

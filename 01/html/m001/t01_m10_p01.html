Purpose

The fan containment case serves as a shroud for the fan blades and is designed to contain a blade in the event of a separation. The system is made up of an abradable surface in the fan blade path, a honeycomb structure and several layers of Kevlar wrapping. The containment case bolts to the aft fan case and is supported by four pairs of tie rods between the aft fan case and the compressor front frame. 60 outlet guide vanes direct air flow into the nacelle and are sealed by spacers in the aft fan case and the vane inner support which prevents air leakage around the guide vanes. The splitter nose directs air, splitting the airflow into the bypass flow and into the engine core.

Functional description
  • When there is an increase in PLA, or throttle advance, the N2 throttle cam will compress the speeder spring and places an increased force on top of the flyweights.
  • This desired or reference speed now exceeds the centrifugal force of the flyweights, or actual speed, and the flyweights fall in.
  • The governor is sensing an underspeed condition and will open the metering valve.
  • Additional fuel is supplied to the engine and N2 increases.
    • This causes the flyweights to rotate faster, causing them to move outward.
    • When they achieve a parallel position, actual speed equals the reference speed, the system will again be in a null condition.
  • A decrease in speed reference will cause the flyweights to move outward and the governor will sense an overspeed condition, reducing the metering valve position, causing N2 to decrease and moving the flyweights back to the null position.

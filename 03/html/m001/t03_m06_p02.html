Operation
  • At idle, the engine speed is controlled by the T2C sensor when the PLA is in the idle position.
  • At 15 to 18 Degrees PLA, the N2 cam is not in contact with the cam follower, but is controlled by the T2C servo and the idle adjustment screw.
    • As outside air temperature decreases, the T2C servo pulls the idle speed lever away from the cam follower, reducing the force on the speeder spring.
    • This causes the flyweights to move outward which causes the metering valve to close down.
  • On a hot day, T2C increases the force on the speeder spring, causing them to move inward and moving the metering valve open.
  • A click type ground adjustment screw can be adjusted to regulate the idle speed value within a tolerance band for a given temperature.
  • Minimum idle is set at 56.9%.

Operation
  • As fuel flow changes, the differential across the metering valve will be affected.
    • The valve is actually a rotary type valve, changing the orifice size as it rotates.
  • Fuel into the MFC is at a constant pressure and the pressure differential across the metering valve is sensed by the fuel metering system.
    • The system will maintain a constant 36 psi differential through the use of a bypass valve, increasing or decreasing fuel flow back to the fuel pump inlet as changes in metering valve position occurs.
  • During engine operation, there is a constant flow of fuel from the bypass valve in the MFC to the pump inlet.
  • The overspeed protection for N2 is part of the fuel metering system.
  • A flyweight, rotating from the N2 input shaft into the MFC, will cause the bypass valve to open fully and stop further acceleration beyond 18,400 rpm.
  • When the Power Lever Angle (PLA) is placed in the "off" position, the stopcock valve is closed causing flow to stop, fully opening the bypass and returning all fuel flow to the pump inlet.
    • This prevents any pressure buildup during shut-down.

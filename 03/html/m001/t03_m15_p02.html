Operation
  • The N1 control amplifier is pre-programed at the factory or during overhaul with a schedule of varying values of reference N1 at different PLA and N2 conditions.
  • The throttle cam within the MFC is profiled to provide an N1 reference speed that is in accordance with the curves that where pre-programed.
  • Movement of the PLA changes the N1 throttle cam, which represents PLA, and is translated by the Linear Variable Differential Transducer (LVDT) in the MFC into an electrical signal that is sent to the N1 amplifier.
  • During operation of the engine, the N1 amplifier continously compares the actual N1 with the reference N1 for the given PLA and T2.
    • As long as the actual N1 is less than the reference N1 (lower power settings), the engine is controlled by the N2 governer.
    • When the actual N1 exceeds the referenced N1, the control amplifier supplies an electrical error signal to the torqumotor in the MFC.
  • The torquemotor assumes control of the metering valve position and regulates the fuel flow in order to maintain actual N1 at a value that is in line with the factory-referenced N1 at the given PLA and T2.
  • A mechanical lockout prevents the N1 control system from influencing the metering valve at speeds below 79.1%.

Operation
  • The APR system is made operational by placing the APR switch on the engine control panel to the ARM position.
    • In this position a logic system provided by the aircraft begins to monitor the operation of the engines.
  • If, during takeoff, the logic system detects a failure of one engine, the APR system will reset the N1 reference schedule in the torquemotor by 2.3%.
    • This increases the N1 by approximately 169 rpm.
  • After takeoff is initiated, the system will remain armed for five minutes.
    • This ensures that the system will not effect engine operation in other phases of flight.

Operation
  • Within the MFC, fuel is ported to the system through a variable orifice that reduces the pressure on top of the servo piston.
    • This fuel is then sent externally to the T2C sensor where it will then pass across another variable orifice within the T2C sensor.
  • A pressure drop occurs across the orifice and the fuel is returned to the MFC to the lower side of the servo piston.
  • As compressor inlet temperature increases, the pressure of the helium within the finger probe increases.
    • This raises the pressure in the sensor bellows, expanding it, and the area of the orifice is reduced.
    • This restricts the fuel flow across the variable orifice in the T2C sensor and will cause the pressure on top of the sensor piston in the MFC to increase, forcing the piston down and increasing idle speed.
  • As temperature decreases, the helium pressure in the probe and bellows decreases, contracting it, opening the variable orifice in the T2C sensor.
    • The restriction across the orifice is reduced and that in turn reduces the pressure on top of the sensor piston, allowing spring tension under the piston to force it up, reducing idle speed.

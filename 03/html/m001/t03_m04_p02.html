Operation
  • The N2 input to the MFC is transmitted through the power takeoff assembly, which is splined to the compressor rotor, radial drive shaft, the AGB, the fuel pump and finally the coupling shaft .
  • The coupling shaft rotates two bevel gears and the flyweights.
    • The centrifugal force caused by the rotary motion forces the flyweights to move outwards.
    • However, the outward movement is opposed by the force of the speeder spring that is being compressed by the cam follower and the N2 throttle cam.
  • When the PLA, or reference speed and the actual speed from the N2 input are equal, the flyweights will be in a null, or vertical position and fuel flow is maintained constant.
  • Any movement of the flyweights positions a pilot valve in the N2 governor servo which controls the opening and closing of the metering valve.

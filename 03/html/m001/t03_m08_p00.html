Identification

  • Movement of the throttles in the cockpit result in corresponding rotation of the throttle shaft located on the MFC through a cable assembly that is mounted at 12 o'clock on the engine.
    • This rotation increases or decreases engine rpm.
  • The PLA is a measure of the angular movement of the power lever in relation to the fuel shutoff point marked on the indexing plate on the MFC.
    • The power lever is capable of angular movement of 100 degrees.
  • Movement is marked as a series of dwell bands on the indexing plate. They are:
    • Fuel shutoff - 0-3 degrees
    • Idle - 15-18 degrees
    • Maximum - 97-100 degrees

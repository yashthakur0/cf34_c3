Purpose
  • Below 4600 feet the idle is controlled through the idle adjustment screw and the T2C sensor.
    • As altitude increase above 4600, P0 and temperature decrease, and the air is less dense and if the system where to remain on T2C control, it would drive the idle to a low speed, reducing bleed and acceleration capabilities.
    • To ensure proper operation, the system now is under P0 control.
  • The P0, or ambient pressure is ported to the P0 bellows and as pressure decreases the bellows will contract, opening the ball valve and reducing the pressure on the top of the reset piston.
    • This causes the piston to move up which will move the idle reset cam clockwise.
    • It will then make contact with the idle speed lever, moving it away from the idle adjustment screw.
    • This places more demand on the speeder spring, which increases rpm.

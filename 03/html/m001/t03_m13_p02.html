Operation
  • A 3D cam within the accel/decel computer system in the MFC receives the N2 and T2C inputs.
  • The N2 input causes rotational movement of the cam and the T2C input causes axial movement of the cam.
    • The resultant movement of the cam is transmitted to the pilot valve in the VG system servo through the cam follower.
  • An increase or decrease in speed will cause the 3D cam to rotate causing the cam follower to ride along ridges and valleys on the cam.
  • The follower is connected to the pilot valve and depending on the rotation of the cam, will position the pilot valve to add fuel pressure to the rod or head fittings of both VG actuators simultaneously.
  • Since the VG feedback signal is connected to the cam follower, the movement of the feedback signal will move the pilot valve either up or down and return the system to a null position, and movement stops.

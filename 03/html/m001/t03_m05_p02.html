Operation
  • During a rapid throttle advance to a higher power setting, the N2 governer senses an underspeed condition and schedules a large increase in fuel flow.
  • The engine, operating at a low rpm, cannot handle this amount of fuel and can stall.
  • The acel/decel computer controls the increase of fuel flow by controlling the maximum and minimum fuel flow to the engine combustor for any given value of core engine speed.
  • As the engine speed approaches the set point, the system will anticipate and reduce the fuel flow, slowing the acceleration rate near the set point, ensuring stable speed governing.
  • On a rapid deceleration, an overspeed condition is sensed by the N2 governing system and schedules a large decrease in fuel flow.
  • There is a rapid deceleration and the acel/decel computer slows the rate of deceleration as it approaches the set point, preventing an undershoot.

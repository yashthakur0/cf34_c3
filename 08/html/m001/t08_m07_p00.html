Identification

  • The engine has eighteen fuel injectors.
  • Each fuel injector is mounted in a combustion chamber port.
    • In the port, it is positioned by a locating pin on the injector flange and held with a B-nut.
    • One end of the fuel injector is inserted into the swirler ferrule in the combustor liner.
    • The other end is connected to the fuel manifold hose end.
  • Internally, the injector consists of the following:
    • Primary and secondary orifices that open to the tip of the injector
    • Primary and secondary fuel flow passages
    • Primary and secondary check valves
  • The eighteen fuel injectors are numbered clockwise from one to eighteen, starting with the first injector to the right of the 12 o’clock position (aft looking forward).
  • There are two types of fuel injectors mounted on the engine.
    • The No. 13 fuel injector that is located near the MFC at approx 8 o’clock position on the engine, has a horizontal orientation.
    • The remaining seventeen injectors have an angular orientation.

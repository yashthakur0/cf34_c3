Operation

  • During low power settings (fuel pressure between manifold pressure and combustion chamber above approx 20 psid), the primary check valve in the primary fuel flow passage opens and allows fuel to flow through the primary orifice.
    • The fuel reaches the injector tip and exits as a single cone of atomized spray into the combustor liner.
    • When the fuel manifold pressure reduces below approx 20 psid, the primary check valve closes and prevents the fuel from dripping into the combustion liner.
  • At above-idle power settings, the increased pressure in the fuel manifold causes the secondary check valve in the secondary fuel flow passage to open.
    • This forces the fuel through the secondary orifice.
    • The fuel reaches the injector tip and exits as a secondary cone of atomized spray.
    • This secondary spray cone surrounds the primary spray cone.

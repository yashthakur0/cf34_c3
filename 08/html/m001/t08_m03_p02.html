Operation

  • The aircraft ejector boost pumps supply fuel from the aircraft fuel tank to the fuel pump through the fuel feed shutoff valve.
    • The fuel enters the fuel pump at a pressure of approximately 15 psi.
  • In the fuel pump, the rotating impeller element in the boost stage increases the inlet fuel pressure by approx 65 psi.
  • This pressurized fuel flows out of the fuel pump and branches out into two parts.
    • One part flows through the heat exchanger and the fuel filter and back into the fuel pump into the primary stage.
    • The other part flows back into the fuel pump into the secondary stage through the secondary stage inlet screen.
  • The rotating gear element in the primary stage increases the pressure of the fuel and supplies it to the MFC.
    • A relief valve, provided between the inlet and discharge sides of the primary stage, opens if the fuel pressure increases above approx 750 psi due to downstream clogging.
  • The rotating gear element in the secondary stage increases the pressure of the fuel and supplies it to the aircraft fuel tank for providing the motive flow for the ejector boost pumps.
    • A relief valve, provided between the inlet and discharge sides of the secondary stage opens if the fuel pressure increases above approx 650 psi due to downstream clogging.
  • As the AGB drives the fuel pump, the driving torque is transmitted to the coupling shaft installed at the aft end of the pump, causing it to rotate.
    • The rotating coupling shaft supplies the MFC with the N2 input signal.

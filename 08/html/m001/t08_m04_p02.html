Operation

  • Fuel at a boost pressure of approx 80 psi passes through the heat exchanger and enters the filter housing from the top.
    • The fuel then flows down into the filter housing and enters the filter element from the outside.
    • As it flows towards the center of the filter element, the dirt particles get trapped in the layers of paper.
    • From the center of the filter element, the fuel is routed back to the top of the housing and out to the fuel pump through an external fuel line.
  • The housing contains a bypass valve that opens when the pressure differential across a clogged filter element exceeds approx 22 to 27 psi.
    • When the bypass valve opens, the fuel bypasses the filter element and flows directly to the fuel pump.
    • A red warning button on top of the filter housing pops up to indicate that fuel is bypassing the fuel element.
    • Before the bypass valve opens (pressure differential = approx 16 to 19 psi), the impending bypass switch on the filter housing sends a warning signal to the cockpit.
  • Airframe manufacturer-supplied thermocouple:
    • Installed into the fuel temperature sensing port on top of the filter housing.
    • Provides fuel filter inlet temperature to the cockpit.
  • Before the filter element can be removed, the drain port at the bottom of the filter bowl has to be opened to drain the filter bowl.

Functional description

The air:

  • Enters the core engine and passes through the 14 stage HPC.
    • Air pressure is increased in preparation for combustion.
  • Enters the combustion chamber through:
    • Dome swirlers, where it is mixed with the fuel.
    • Dilution holes surrounding the inner and outer liner walls, to help control the chamber exhaust temperature.
  • High velocity air exits from the combustion chamber
    • Passes through the stage one and stage two HPT.
    • Driving the HPT and HPC which it is splinned to.
  • Passes through a four stage LPT , which drives the fan.
  • Exhausts the engine, contributing approx 20% of the total engine thrust.

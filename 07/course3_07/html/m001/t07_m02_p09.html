Operation

The valve receives unregulated air from the seventh stage bleed port on the compressor case and maintains a designed supply pressure to the seals by the use of a piston, spring and variable orifice. The unregulated air passes through the valve, which reduces the pressure to approximately 30 psia supplying the A and C sump seals.

When the pressure downstream of the regulating valve exceeds 30 psia, the force on the air side of the piston exceeds the spring force on the ambient side and the piston will move, reducing the orifice area and reducing the pressure. Conversely, if the pressure falls below 30 psia, the spring force will open up the orifice area and increase the pressure.

A cover with vent holes is bolted to the valve housing and allows for ambient air to enter the spring side of the piston. As aircraft altitude increases, ambient pressure decreases and allows the piston to maintain to designed pressure to the seals, regardles of the altitude.

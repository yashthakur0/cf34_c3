Functional Description

Leakage from the #1 carbon seal will present itself in different areas. Externally, indications include:

  • Oil dripping from the overboard drain, or gang drain.
  • Oil dripping from the fan static seal 6 o’clock weep holes.
  • Oil puddling in the static seal at 6 o’clock.
  • Heavy oil wetting at the inner ends of the exit guide vanes.

Internally, there also will be evidence of leakage, which would require a borescope to investigate these areas:

  • Oil dripping from the forward edge of the carbon seal assembly,
  • Heavy wetting on the #1 bearing support plate,
  • Oil leakage from the aft flange of the carbon seal,
  • All the above symptoms may be combined with high oil consumption.

Further isolation of high oil consumption caused by the #1 seal may indicate that there is oil wetting at the aft flange of the carbon seal assembly with oil puddling in the fan case and no evidence at the gang drain. This would indicate that the assembly o-ring is the cause for the leakage. Replace the o-ring. The remaining symptoms of a seal leak indicate leakage past one or both seal element. Replace the #1 carbon seal.

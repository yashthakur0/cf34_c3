Operation

  • High pressure air from the air starter valve enters the inlet of the turbine stator
    • Flows across the blades of the turbine wheel causing the turbine wheel to turn at high speed.
    • Used air is let out of the ATS through the ports in the starter housing.
  • The reduction gear assembly
    • Converts the high speed, low torque energy of the turbine wheel into low speed, high torque energy and transmits it to the sprag clutch.
    • Sends a speed signal to the monopole speed sensor.
    • The monopole speed sensor transmits this signal to the speed cutout switch through a cable.
  • The rotating sprag clutch drives the clutch shaft, which rotates the oil pump and the output shaft assembly.
    • The rotating splined output shaft then drives the AGB.
  • When the output shaft reaches a specified speed of rotation, the sprag clutch is over run
    • Output shaft continues to rotate the clutch shaft without rotating the reduction gear assembly.
    • This makes sure that after the start of the engine, the splined output shaft and the clutch shaft continue to be rotated by the drive from the AGB, but the reduction gear assembly does not turn.
  • The rotating oil pump makes sure that the clutch shaft bearings are lubricated by splash-type lubrication.
  • If a malfunction in the sprag clutch occurs, the emergency decoupler in the output shaft assembly disengages the starter from the AGB.
    • In addition, the shear section in the output shaft of the assembly is designed to break if an overtorque condition occurs.
    • The containment shield around the turbine wheel is designed to trap the expelled turbine wheel parts in case of mechanical failure.

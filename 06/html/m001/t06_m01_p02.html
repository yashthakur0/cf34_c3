Operation
  • The ignition system operates under the three kinds of the conditions that follow:
    • Manual Ignition
    • Continuous Ignition
    • Auto-Ignition

Manual Ignition

  • The ignition system for the left and the right engine is the same, but operate independently.
    • In manual mode the ignition systems are controlled from the L-ENG-R/IGNITION Panel.
    • When ignition A or B is selected, the related ignition system is armed and the green ARM light illuminates.
  • With the starting system energized, power is supplied from the set ignition switch/light to the engine start relays and then to the ignition power relays in JB-5.
  • After the JB-5 relay closes, 115 volts AC is supplied to the ignition exciter either from the 115 volts AC ESS Bus or from the 28 volts DC Battery Bus through the static inverter.
    • The related ignition white ON light illuminates along with the A or B ignition green EICAS message.
    • The start sequence is latched and the START switch/light released.
  • When the engine starts and accelerates to the idle speed, the starter cutout switch opens at 55% N2.
  • The start latch relay and the start bleed air relay de-energize, and deactivate ignition.

Continuous Ignition

  • Can be selected to maintain engine combustion in icing conditions or when landing on wet or slush-covered runways.
  • Both the ignition systems are energized on the engines.
  • Selecting the CONT switch/light closes two contactors and puts power on the four relays in JB-5.
    • This causes both plugs on the engines to fire.

Auto-Ignition

  • Activated automatically by the stall protection computer, whenever the aircraft angle of attack results in a disruption of airflow to the engine intakes.
    • Both ignition systems on each engine are energized by closing the two stall protection relays.

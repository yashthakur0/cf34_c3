Operation

The pump, which is geared to the AGB, draws oil from the supply tank through the one supply element and flows across the internal oil filter. If the delta pressure becomes high enough, due to contamination, the impending bypass switch will close and give an indication to the ground crew. Continued contamination will open the bypass around the filter and no indication is given. Oil then flows to the two system pressure sensors and externally to the heat exchanger.

Oil then flows to the B-sump and C-sump lubrication jets. It also flows through the forward face of the pump to the lubrication jets of the AGB and A-Sump. If supply pressure reaches 150 psi, an internal relief valve will open allowing excess oil pressure to bleed directly to output port to the oil tank.

The 6 scavenge elements draw oil from B-Sump, C-Sump, and AGB forward and aft scavenge lines and manifolds the discharge of each internally. The single pump discharge sends the oil to the oil tank.

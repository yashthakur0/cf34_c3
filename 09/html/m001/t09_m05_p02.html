Operation

The oil tank can be serviced by gravity through the filler cap or remotely filled through the remote replenishment system located in the airframe through the remote fill port located on the tank. The scavenged oil returns to the tank from the lube and scavenge pump and passes across the master chip detector, where any metal contamination is captured.

It is then internally routed to the cyclone type deaerator located internally at the top of the tank. There, separated air is vented through the tank pressurizing valve to A-sump and the oil drops into the tank. The pressurizing valve maintains a differential pressure between the tank and A-sump of five psid. This suppresses the formation of vapor within the tank and prevents cavitation of the oil pressurizing element within the lube and scavenge pump at high altitudes. The valve piston contains a bleed hole to vent pressure upon engine shut down. An oil temperature sensor is install through the sensing port and provides the flight crew an indication of oil temperature on EICAS.

Functional Description

A definition of each Core (N2) vibration cause follows:

  • Thermal rotor bow occurs when an engine is shut down and restarted within 2 hours. It is a temporary condition caused by uneven cooling of the HPC or LPT rotors. N2 vibration levels can exceed the 1.7 mil threshold. Engine operation is permitted if the vibration level decreases to a normal level after 10 minutes.
  • Indication system problems include the accelerometer and harness as well as the airframe system components.
  • Internal damage can be from failed hardware, FOD damage to the compressor or high pressure turbine damage. The verification of internal damage will require a borescope inspection of the HPC and HPT.

Identification

  • Observations from the following personnel are important to indicate engine faults:
    • Flight crew
    • Ground crew
  • Flight crew observations include indications such as:
    • Unusual odors in the aircraft.
    • Excessive or abnormal engine or airframe vibrations.
    • Abnormal engine light indications.
    • Fluctuating engine indications such as speed, temperature or pressure.
    • Engine fault indications on the EICAS Primary or Secondary pages.
  • Ground crew observations include indications such as:
    • Fluids leaking from the cowling drain ports or other locations on the engine.
    • Unusual odors coming from the engine, cowling or other areas of the aircraft.
    • Broken engine or cowling parts found on the aircraft or in an area close to the aircraft.
